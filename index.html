About

I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in LSA Astronomy at the University of Michigan, working with Prof. John Monnier and Prof. Steven Cundiff to demonstrate multi-heterodyne interferometry for astronomical applications using electro-optic comb technology.

My broader research interests include optical frequency combs, ultrafast quantum optical technology, quantum spectroscopy, single-photon detector technology, and spin-orbit interactions of light.

Research Interests

During my Ph.D., I established an ultrafast time-resolved measurement technique leveraging optical frequency comb-based sampling technology. This approach enables sub-100 fs timing precision to probe the temporal structure of quantum-entangled ultrafast SPDC photon pairs and demonstrates photonic quantum state engineering.

In my Master’s research, I investigated the spin-Hall effect of light at interfaces, including beam propagation through a Dove prism and reflection at an air-prism interface.
